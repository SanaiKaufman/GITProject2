Status

Purpose:

The Git Status Bar (lower left) shows the current branch, dirty indicators, incoming and outgoing commits. This allows you to see all the changes happening with each file.

Add

Purpose:

Helps gets your documents ready to commit and allows flexibility in choosing which documents to commit.

Step 1:

Go to the source control panel and view the documents. New or changed files have a "U" next to the document title.

Step 2:

Hit the + button to stage the document and the "U" will change to "A".

Commit

Purpose:

To solidify your changes before uploading and provides a Git commit history that you can view.

Step 1:

First you must ensure your pages are staged or "added" first.

Step 2:

Type a message into the message line and click the "commit" button.

Push

Purpose:

When working on files in VS Code, your files will stay on your computer until you push it to the GitHub repository

Step 1:

Click on source control in the left hand panel.

Step 2:

Commit the changes you've made and hit the "..." on the top right corner of the panel to expand the menu.

Step 3:

Select push under the expanded menu. Your changes are now updated in your GitHub repository and is live on your site.

Pull

Purpose

When working on files in GitHub or on another linked device, all of your files will go to GitHub. If you are working on a device that does not have the updated files, you will want to pull it from your repository.

Step 1:

Click on source control in the left hand panel.

Step 2:

Click it the "..." on the top right corner of the panel to expand the menu.

Step 3:

Select pull under the expanded menu. Your changes are now updated in your VS Code.
